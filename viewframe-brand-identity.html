01 — Name

Keep Viewframe.

The name is precise, premium, and secured. No alternative improves on it for this market.

Why it works

Viewframe

  • Two nouns that do double duty — view (what you see through a window) and frame (the architectural element being specified)
  • Reads instantly as a window product; no explanation required
  • Domain secured: viewframe.co.uk
  • One word, easy to say aloud, no spelling ambiguity
  • Works as a company name and as a verb: "Viewframe your extension before you commit"
Name tests

Four tests, four passes

  • Distinction — No direct competitor uses it; not confused with existing brands
  • Legibility — Reads at small sizes, works spoken aloud, no phonetic confusion
  • Extendability — "Viewframe Pro", "Viewframe for Trade" — both read naturally
  • Tone fit — Premium, considered, architectural. Not generic or playful
"Casement, Prospect, Frameside — these are serviceable. Viewframe is already better than all of them, and you own the domain."

02 — Positioning

The most considered
purchase in your home.

Windows are not a utility decision. They're a £20,000–£40,000 commitment that changes how a home looks for thirty years. Viewframe exists at the moment between desire and decision.

The insight

Homeowners feel the weight of window decisions but have no way to test them. They choose from catalogues and swatches, then wait six weeks to see if they were right.

The mechanism

Viewframe lets them upload their home, apply real frame colours and glass styles, and see the result before they spend a penny. AI is the mechanism — confidence is the promise.

The promise

You'll know what your home will look like before the installer arrives. Not a guess, not a sample board, not a salesperson's opinion. A real preview of a real decision.

Brand qualities
1

Trustworthy

We handle a significant financial decision. Every word, every pixel earns or spends trust. We are specific, accurate, and never oversell what the tool can do.

2

Considered

We move at the pace of the decision. No urgency tactics, no countdown timers. We create space for people to think carefully, because that's what the purchase deserves.

3

Enabling

We give people the tools to arrive at their own answer. Viewframe doesn't tell you which windows to pick — it gives you the information to decide for yourself.

4

Premium, not expensive

Premium means quality of experience and clarity of value, not high price or luxury aesthetics. We are rigorous, not luxurious. Precise, not fussy.

Fraunces — Display only

Use Fraunces 300 for: page headlines, section titles, pull quotes, and the wordmark. Never use Fraunces for body copy, button labels, or UI text smaller than 20px. The italic weight is reserved for emotional emphasis — one use per page.

Plus Jakarta Sans — Everything else

Weight 400 for body and navigation. Weight 500 for secondary emphasis. Weight 600 for buttons, section labels (uppercase + tracking), and the wordmark companion. Never bold (700+) in UI elements — it reads as alert severity, not hierarchy.

06 — Voice

Plain. Specific. Warm.

Viewframe is a knowledgeable friend who happens to know everything about windows. Not a salesperson. Not a tech platform. Someone who helps you think clearly before you spend serious money.

Plain

Describe actions and outcomes.

Write what the product does and what the homeowner gains. Never describe internal mechanisms unless they build trust. "AI" is fine as a mechanism; "AI-powered" as a qualifier adds nothing.

Specific

Use real numbers, real names.

Name window types: casement, sash, tilt-and-turn. Name frame materials: uPVC, aluminium, timber. Use real export formats: PDF spec sheet, not "professional report." Specificity earns trust.

Warm

You're on their side.

Acknowledge the weight of the decision. The tone is never formal, never flippant. We understand this matters. We don't rush, we don't push, we help people arrive at their own answer.

UI colour rules
  • App background: #0B1220 (slightly deeper than navy — reduces eye strain)
  • Selected state: bronze border + 20% bronze fill on swatches and options
  • Interactive elements: bronze accent; destructive: #EF4444; success: #22C55E
  • Canvas detection overlays: bronze at 60% opacity, not full saturation
  • Text on dark: white for primary, rgba(255,255,255,0.4) for secondary/disabled
Interaction principles
  • Detection happens automatically — never show a loading spinner for more than 3 seconds
  • "Apply to all" is always visible — most users want consistent windows across the house
  • Undo/reset is always one click away — builds confidence to experiment
  • PDF export should require zero additional input — no modal, no form
  • Share link copies to clipboard silently — confirm with a brief toast, not a modal
